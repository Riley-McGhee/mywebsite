About Me

            I’m Riley McGhee. I’m 15, into tech, and I build things that I actually use—custom keyboards, websites, tools—whatever helps me learn and improve. I’ve made a number of keyboards for others, and I’ve also been experimenting with web development. This site is one of the websites I’ve put together—still a work in progress, but hopefully it’s not too rough around the edges.

What I Do

What I Do Here McG Custom Keyboards isn’t just about putting switches in a case. It’s about building something that fits you. If you want a keyboard that doesn’t just work but feels personal and reflects your setup or style—that’s what I do. Here’s how it works:

  • What size board do you need? Full-size? TKL? 65%? I build to your layout. (What is a layout? The inclusion or exclusion of keys for personalization and taking up less space.)
  • What kind of feel do you want? Smooth, tactile, linear, loud, quiet—whatever suits your hands and habits. (some are better or worse in certain environments, and depending on preference, the "weight" of a spring could change everything)
  • How do you want it to sound?
  • Whether it's a sharp clack, smooth pop, or a deep thock, sound shapes the whole experience.
  • What’s the aesthetic? Monochrome? Retro? Warm wood and brass? I’ll match the board to your space.
  • Got specific interests? Anime, cars, space, minimalism—if it matters to you, I’ll find a way to reflect it.
  • Want matching accessories? I can help you find a cable or deskmat that completes the setup.

Have questions? Want a custom keyboard of your own? Visit the contact page, shoot me an email, and I will reply as soon as possible!
